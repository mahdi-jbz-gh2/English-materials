# Unit 39 — if I knew … / I wish I knew …

Use **if + past** for imagined situations **now**; meaning is present, not past. 

Use **wish + past** to express regret about how things are **now**. 


---

## Pattern table

| Function               | Structure                                  | Example                                                                          |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Unreal condition (now) | **if + past**, **… would/could/might + V** | *If I **knew** his number, I **would phone** him.*                               |
| Polite Advice / imagined role | **If I were you**, …                   | *If I **were** you, I wouldn’t buy that coat.*                                   |
| Wish for now           | **I wish + past**                          | *I **wish** I **knew** Paul’s number.*                                           |
| Could (ability)        | **wish + could** / **if … could**          | *It would be useful if he **could** (drive).* / *I **wish I could** help you.*   |
| Were vs. was           | **were** also possible after **if/wish**   | *I wish Anna **were/was** here.*                                                 |

> Don’t use **would** in the if-clause or after **wish** for states:

> ✅ *If I **were** rich, I **would** travel.* ✖ *If I **would be** rich…*;

> ✅ *I wish I **had** something to read.* ✖ *I wish I **would have**…* 

---

## Common Mistakes

* ✖ *If I **would know*** → ✔ *If I **knew*** (no **would** in if-part). 
* ✖ *I wish I **would be** here now* → ✔ *I wish I **was/were** here now.* 
* ✖ *If I **was** you…* (informal OK), more careful style: **If I were you**.
